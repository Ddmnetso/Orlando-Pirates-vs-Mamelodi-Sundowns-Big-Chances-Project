# Orlando-Pirates-vs-Mamelodi-Sundowns-Big-Chances-Project
Football analytics project examining Orlando Pirates' big chance creation and conversion over 5 PSL seasons, testing whether the rise in big chances missed correlates with increased chance creation or reflects a genuine finishing decline.

## Question
 
A [recent video posted on social media](https://www.tiktok.com/@mikemortontalksfootball/video/7618581689178066197) suggested that Orlando Pirates have a finishing problem, they have created the most chances in the league but missed the most *big chances*. The video tracked an increase in big chances missed over the past 5 seasons but did not account for chance creation and big chances created over that same period.
 
## Hypothesis
 
Are Orlando Pirates missing more big chances **because they are creating more**, or are these events mutually exclusive — meaning there has been no significant rise in big chances created, but a significant increase in big chances missed?
 
## Data Sources
 
- [Sofascore](https://www.sofascore.com)
- [FBref](https://fbref.com)
 
### Metrics at Focus
 
| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Big Chances | Total big chances created per season |
| Big Chances Missed | Total big chances not converted |
| Big Chances Missed % | Ratio of missed to created |
| Big Chances Per Game | Average big chances created per match |

## Observations
![](https://github.com/Ddmnetso/Orlando-Pirates-vs-Mamelodi-Sundowns-Big-Chances-Project/blob/main/Screenshot%202026-03-20%20153938.png)
### Big Chance Creation
 
Claims of Pirates missing a lot of big chances are supported, however the **rate at which these chances are being created has also grown**:
 
| Season | Big Chances | Games | Per Game |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2024/2025 | 70 | 28 | 2.50 |
| 2025/2026 (current) | 73 | 21 | 3.48 |
 
The current season has already matched last season's total in significantly fewer games, pointing to a clear upward trend in chance creation.
 
### Big Chances Missed
 
Over the past 3 seasons there has been a significant spike in big chances missed. However, in the 21 games played so far this season, the **big chances missed rate has remained consistent at approximately 71%** — in line with the 2024/2025 season — suggesting no meaningful decline in finishing quality.
 
### Contextual: Pirates vs. Sundowns
 
In both the current season and 2024/2025, Orlando Pirates have actively challenged Mamelodi Sundowns for the league title. How do the key metrics differ?
 
| Club | Big Chances | Missed | Missed % | Conversion Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mamelodi Sundowns | 81 | 39 | 48.15% | ~5 in 10 |
| Orlando Pirates | 70 | 50 | 71.42% | ~3 in 10 |
 
Sundowns are significantly more clinical in front of goal. For every 10 big chances, Sundowns convert at least 5 while Pirates convert roughly 3.
 
---
 
## Takeaway
 
It is statistically correct that Pirates are not clinical in front of goal relative to their title rivals. However, two things are also true:
 
1. **They are creating more big chances** - at a higher rate than any previous season in the sample.
2. **They are just as clinical as the previous season** - the missed % has not worsened.
 
The growth in big chances missed therefore **directly correlates with the growth in big chances created**, not a decline in finishing ability.
 
A more complete analysis of their title challenge would require examining the frequency of dropped points, how those points are lost, and the relationship between those moments and the chance data above.
